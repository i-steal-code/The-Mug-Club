Matcha latte (base) 12oz cup


Ingredients:
* Medium sized ice cubes x ~200g
* Milk 3-4% fat x ~108 g
* Naoki Matcha powder x 1 tsp
* Heavy cream x 1 tbsp
* 12oz cup, lid & straw
* Brown sugar (ORIGINAL)
* Strawberry puree 
* Honey buttercream 


Ingredient prep:
* Crush ice cubes into medium/large size to ensure it can fit in the cup comfortably
* Prep cup with mug club label, customer name and order on bottom
* Fill cup with ice and set in freezer


Matcha cloud:
1. Sift 1 tsp of matcha powder into a mixing cup
2. Add ½ tbsp milk and 1 tbsp heavy cream 
3. Stir gently to incorporate matcha into cream
4. Whisk until cloud texture forms
5. Continue whisking until needed by assembly


* Step 3 is to ensure the matcha doesnt float on the cream and fly up when mixer is turned on
* DO NOT USE BAMBOO WHISK as you will kill it with the fat content of the milk and may potentially contaminate your whisk (unless if you plan to only use the bamboo whisk on milky matcha from now on) 
* Use a moderately tall and small base mixing cup to ensure everything doesn't just splash out 


final assembly:
1. Pour milk into ice-filled cup (to your own discretion)
2. Pour fresh matcha cloud over the top
3. Lid and serve


* If you are feeling generous the milk goes all the way up to the line
* For a more even spread of matcha, pour matcha cloud over an inverted spoon 
* Scrape matcha cup clean using spoon

Matcha cloud variations
ORIGINAL matcha cloud (according to the menu…)
* Just make the regular matcha cloud but with 1 tbsp brown sugar this time 
* Will be very thick, if too thick, can use a little milk to loosen it up abit (up to your own discretion)

We make the cream first


Thicc cream (do with cold ingredients):
1. Whisk Egg yolks + sugar  in mixing bowl
2. Mix in cream cheese
3. Mix in heavy cream
4. Whip halfway and add whatever the hell was supposed to be added
5. Whip till peaks form


Wet biscuits + layer tiramisu:
1. Make very concentrated matcha using matcha powder (300ml ish)
2. Dip ladyfinger cookies in matcha lightly and lay in dish till 1st layer of wet biscuit is formed
3. Dollop half the cream on top of wet biscuit layer
4. Repeat step 2-3 to complete 4 layered tiramisu
5. Dust top of tiramisu with remaining matcha powder
6. Put tiramisu and tray in freezer overnight 
7. Thaw slightly before serving

Honey buttercream 30g
Ingredients: (3 parts, by weight)
* Salted butter x 1
* Heavy cream x 1
* Honey x 1
* Brown sugar x a pinch
* Salt x a pinch
Honey buttercream: (batch made)
1. Brown 1 part butter into saucepan with a pinch of salt and brown sugar
2. Cool down while mixing 1 part heavy cream into browned butter
3. Add 2 part honey (preferably crystalised)
4. Whisk until thick & frothy
5. Refrigerate to preserve crystalline & cream structure

Bottlenecks: 
1. Matcha cloud making (live) takes ~50s-60s to complete
2. Finding of cup, pouring of milk, lidding & straw can be expedited
With 2 matcha cloud stations and 1 assembly station, here is how the workflow would go from order to serve (by the second)


Current workflow: 2 matcha stations 1 assembly station


Time/s (estimate)
	Action 
	0
	Order taken, retrieve cup for assembly
	5
	Pour ice


Matcha cloud station 1 and 2 staggered output
	15
	Pour milk
	25
	Pour matcha cloud 
	32
	Scrape clean


Lid the cup
	35
	serve
	

The assembly process is actually quite quick, and simulated to be ~30s given all components prepped adequately. Bottleneck mainly lies in the matcha cloud production and the physical limit of how fast one can pour everything into the cup without mistakes. To truly hit the 30s per cup rate, it would require a very well trained assembly barista and 3 matcha cloud stations just spam producing matcha clouds (~20s /matcha cloud). 


An alternative to scaling up the quality of the production is to simply reduce the number of steps. Ice and milk can be pre-portioned before assembling (get a slave to portion milk and ice into cups) to completely erase portioning mistakes and spillage issues. Matcha cloud cannot have any steps skipped as any steps skipped can severely impact quality


So what does this all mean? 
In the 2 slots of 60min before and after class cohort time, we can potentially make 200 cups at max capacity (with a little time to spare) if everyone works in flow state for the entire time. Our current limits are 200 cups at max with a 3-4 man kitchen and 2 to take orders/handle miscellaneous tasks.
